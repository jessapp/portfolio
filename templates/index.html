With a background in the arts, I strive to create elegant software solutions

After graduating Tulane University with a degree in Digital Media Production, I spent several years working as a film producer and editor in the New York City startup scene.

While contracting for a company which creates video coding tutorials, I found a previously undiscovered passion for programming. I learned that code is simply another form of artistic expression. After months of self-study, I decided to pursue software engineering full-time.

I recently attended the Software Engineering Fellowship at Hackbright Academy, where I learned font-end and back-end engineering skills, as well as computer science fundamentals. During that time, I built a web application called Book Bingo from the ground-up.

I aim to bring my creative problem-solving background to my software engineering pursuits, and to constantly learn new skills and technologies.

Book Bingo

Book Bingo is a full-stack web application which allows users to create customizable bingo boards in which each square represents a genre. Upon reading a book that fits one of those genres, the user can enter the book's information into the corresponding field. When a user has read five books in a row, they get bingo. Users can set personal reading goals by playing on their own, or share boards with friends.
